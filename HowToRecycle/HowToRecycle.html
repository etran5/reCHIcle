How to Recycle

"The separation and collection of materials that otherwise would be considered waste, the processing and remanufacturing of these items into new products, and the use of the recycled products to complete the cycle."

Materials That You CAN Recycle

Recycling is an extremely important step towards improving the state of our environment and cleaning the world around us. Everyday consumers in developed countries create a massive amounts of waste and the ways that are used to take care of it are not always the most environmentally friendly. Generally our waste in America, if not recycled, is either placed in a landfill or incinerated. Much of the waste we create is plastic which can take hundreds to thousands of years to decompose. Also the process of burning our trash can not only creates toxic fumes in the air we breath but it also creates greater amounts of greenhouse gases. By recycling, we can reduce the amount of waste flowing into landfills.

1. Plastic containers: bottles and containers with the numbers 1, 2, 3, 4, 5 or 7
2. Glass: jars and bottles
3. Metal: aluminum, tin, or steel cans, foil and pie tins
4. Cartons: milk cartons, juice cartons and aseptic containers
5. Paper: flattened cardboard, office paper, file folders, magazines, catalogs, newspapers, junk mail, telephone books, etc.

How You Should Clean Your Recycleable Materials

Generally, materials that are going to be recycled should emptied of any contents such as liquids or foods. Then any grease or extra things should be rinsed out and placed into a recycleable bag (paper bags should be used rather than plastic groccery bags)

Get to know your plastics

Poly(ethylene terephthalate): PETE or PET plastic is typically a clear material that is considered a safer plastic to use however it is best to avoid it because of its bacteria accumulation.
High-density Polyethylene: HDPE, typically a opaque material, is considered to be a safer plastic to use out of the 7 plastics.
Poly(vinyl cloride): V or PVC is one of the less environmentally friendly plastics that contains phthalates, a group of chemicals that can lead to potential health risks. Cooking with or burning this material is highly discouraged.
Low-density Polyethylene: LDPE is considered a safer plastic to use and is slowly becoming more accepted by recycling programs.
Polypropylene: Polypropylene is a recyclable thermoplastic polymer that is considered versatile because it is categorized as a plastic and fiber.
Polystyrene: PS is one of the least environmentally friendly plastics of the 7, it is extremely hard to recycle and it is highly not recommended to burn due to leaching of potentially toxic chemicals.
This plastic should be avoided due to the possibility of containing BPA, linked to problems such as infertility of hyperactivity.
